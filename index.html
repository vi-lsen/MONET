At the age of 5, young Claude began to paint. He filled his textbooks with sketches of people and caricatures of his teachers. In his hometown, he even became famous for this. The father hoped very much that his son would continue his business, but alas. But the artist was supported by his mother.

The painting "Woman in a Green Dress" brought recognition for the first time to Monet. The paintings were painted in 1866 and featured his future wife Camilla Donsier. By the way, Camille was also a model for other artists - Renoir and Edouard Manet.

In 1872, Claude Monet painted a view from the window of the harbor of Le Havre in France. This work was very poorly received by critics and the public. Soon, the painting became the beginning of the Impressionist movement. It became very popular and spread to music as well.

Claude Monet has a whole series of oil paintings. And they are exhibited all over the world in top museums. The most amazing thing is that Monet's eyesight has deteriorated greatly due to cataracts and he could hardly understand what he was drawing. But it was during this difficult period that he created most of these masterpieces. The most famous are 'Nymphs' and 'Water Lilies'.
